Why this dataset?

Choosing temperature datasets for building models is a good choice for several reasons:

  1. Relevance and Applicability: Temperature is a fundamental variable in many fields such as meteorology, climatology, agriculture, energy, health, among others. Therefore, working with temperature data can be relevant and applicable in a wide range of situations and problems.
  2. Data Availability: Temperature data are often widely available and relatively easy to obtain, whether through weather stations, environmental sensors, historical records, or other sources. This facilitates the construction of robust and comprehensive datasets for analysis and modeling.
  3. Variety of Applications: Models based on temperature data can be used for a variety of purposes, such as predicting future weather, analyzing long-term climate trends, optimizing heating and cooling systems, preventing weather-related diseases, forecasting energy demand, among others.
  4. Intuitive Interpretation: Temperature is a familiar and easily interpretable measure for most people. This makes models based on temperature data more accessible and understandable to a wide audience, which can facilitate the communication of model results and conclusions.
  5. Ease of Visualization: Temperature data can be effectively visualized using graphs, heat maps, and other visualization techniques, which can help identify patterns, trends, and anomalies intuitively.

In summary, choosing temperature datasets for building models can be a good choice due to their relevance, availability, variety of applications, intuitive interpretation, and ease of visualization.
